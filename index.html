Getting started

WareIQ Shipping — Setup Guide

Complete these 9 steps to activate your WareIQ Shipping account, configure settings, and book your first shipment.

~10 minutes 9 steps
Step 1 of 9

Create Your Account

Register at shipping.wareiq.com with your business email. Your Client Code and login credentials are generated at this step.

1
Go to shipping.wareiq.com and click Sign Up
Open the portal in your browser. Click Sign Up on the login page.
2
Enter your business details
Fill in Full Name, Business Email, Mobile Number, and create a Password. Use the email address that will be your primary account contact.
3
Verify your email
Check your inbox for a verification email from WareIQ. Click the link to activate. Your unique Client Code is generated at this point.
FieldNotes
Full Name RequiredAppears on your account and correspondence.
Business Email RequiredUse a company domain if possible. This becomes your login.
Mobile Number RequiredIndian mobile number. Used for OTP and support callbacks.
Password RequiredMinimum 8 characters with a number and special character.
Tip: Use a shared mailbox like shipping@yourbrand.com if multiple team members need account access — WareIQ Shipping does not support multiple logins on one account.
Step 2 of 9

Complete KYC Details

KYC verification unlocks wallet recharge, label generation, and courier access. Have your PAN card and GST certificate ready before starting.

1
Navigate to Settings > KYC Details
After logging in, click Settings in the left sidebar and select KYC Details.
2
Enter your PAN and verify
Type your PAN exactly as on your card. Click Verify — the system auto-fills your legal name from the PAN database.
3
Enter your GSTIN and fetch details
Enter your 15-digit GSTIN and click Fetch. Business name, registered address, and taxpayer type are auto-populated from the GST portal.
4
Upload supporting documents
Upload a clear scan of your PAN card and GST certificate. Accepted: JPG, PNG, PDF. Max 2 MB each.
5
Submit and await review
Click Submit. KYC review completes within 24 hours on business days. You will receive an email confirmation once approved.
FieldNotes
PAN Number Required10-character alphanumeric. Must match the legal entity operating the business.
GSTIN Required15-digit GST ID. Required for GST-compliant invoices.
Business Type RequiredAuto-filled from GST portal — Proprietorship, Pvt Ltd, LLP, etc.
Registered Address RequiredAuto-filled from GST portal. Must match your official address.
Bank Account Optional at KYCAdded separately for refund processing. Not required at initial KYC.
PAN type matters: Proprietorship accounts should submit the owner's personal PAN. Private Limited and LLP accounts must submit the company PAN — not a director's personal PAN.
Step 3 of 9

Accept Terms & Conditions

Review and accept the WareIQ Shipping Service Agreement. This is a one-time step that unlocks full platform access after KYC approval.

1
Open the Terms & Conditions prompt
After KYC is approved, a Terms modal appears on your next login. You can also reach it directly via Settings > Terms & Conditions.
2
Read and accept the agreement
Scroll through the service agreement. Tick the acceptance checkbox and click I Agree. A timestamped confirmation is sent to your registered email.
What the agreement covers: Courier liability limits (typically capped at declared invoice value), prohibited item categories, billing dispute timelines, and NDR handling obligations. Keep the confirmation email.
Step 4 of 9

Recharge Your Wallet

WareIQ Shipping uses a prepaid wallet. Funds are deducted per shipment at label generation. You cannot generate labels with a zero balance.

1
Go to Billing > Recharge Wallet
Click Billing in the left sidebar, then select Recharge Wallet. Your current balance is shown at the top.
2
Enter the recharge amount
Minimum recharge is Rs. 1,000. For a new account, Rs. 5,000–10,000 is a reasonable starting balance to cover your first batch of orders.
Minimum: Rs. 1,000
3
Complete payment
Pay via UPI, Net Banking, or Credit Card. Balance updates within 5 minutes. A GST invoice for the recharge is auto-generated and sent to your email.
Low-balance alerts: Set a minimum balance threshold in Settings > Notifications to receive an email when your wallet falls below a set amount. Recommended: cost of 2–3 days of average shipments.
Step 5 of 9

Review Shipping Rates

Shipping charges depend on courier partner, weight slab, and delivery zone. Review your rate card before creating orders to avoid billing surprises.

1
Navigate to Settings > Shipping Rates
Click Settings in the left sidebar and select Shipping Rates to view the full rate card for all activated couriers.
2
Understand the zone-weight pricing structure
Rates are a base charge (first 500g) plus an incremental charge per additional 500g. Zones A–E correspond to delivery distance from your pickup pincode: Zone A (local) is cheapest, Zone E (remote/North-East) is highest.
3
Use the Rate Calculator
The built-in Rate Calculator on this page estimates cost for any origin pincode, destination pincode, and package weight. Run a few test shipments before going live.
Do this before your first order
ZoneCoverage
Zone AWithin the same city as your pickup address
Zone BNeighbouring states / metro-to-metro corridors
Zone CRest of India
Zone DJammu & Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand
Zone ENorth-East states, Andaman, Lakshadweep
Volumetric weight: Couriers charge on the higher of actual weight vs. volumetric weight. Formula: (L × W × H in cm) ÷ 5000. For large, light packages — apparel in boxes, cushions — always check volumetric weight first.
Step 6 of 9

Add Pickup Address

Your pickup address is where couriers collect packages. Add your warehouse or dispatch location before creating any orders.

1
Go to Settings > Pickup Addresses
Click Settings in the left sidebar and select Pickup Addresses. Click + Add New Pickup Address.
2
Enter full address details
Fill in all address fields carefully. The pincode determines available couriers and your shipping zone matrix — an incorrect pincode means wrong rates.
3
Set as default (single location)
Toggle Set as Default Pickup Address. This pre-populates on all new orders. You can add multiple addresses and switch per order.
4
Save and verify serviceability
Click Save. The system checks courier serviceability at your pincode. Couriers that do not service your location are excluded from auto-allocation.
FieldNotes
Address Name RequiredInternal label — e.g. "Main Warehouse" or "Delhi Dispatch". Not printed on labels.
Contact Person RequiredThe person couriers should call at this location on pickup days.
Contact Number RequiredMobile number at the pickup location. Used by the courier driver on the day.
Address Line 1 & 2 RequiredFull street address. Be precise — courier drivers use this for route planning.
Pincode Required6-digit pincode. Controls zones, rates, and courier availability. Double-check this.
Pickup Cut-off Time OptionalTime by which packages must be ready for same-day pickup per courier SLA.
Multiple locations: You can add up to 10 pickup addresses — useful for brands dispatching from multiple warehouses or sub-brand locations. Each address has independent courier serviceability.
Step 7 of 9

Connect Sales Channels

Integrate your storefronts so orders sync automatically into WareIQ Shipping. Manual order creation is always available, but channel integration removes data entry at scale.

1
Go to Settings > Channel Integration
Click Settings in the left sidebar and select Channel Integration to see all available integration options.
2
Select your storefront or marketplace
Click the channel tile. Native integrations (Shopify, WooCommerce, Magento) use API credentials. Marketplace integrations (Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho) connect via OAuth.
3
Enter credentials or complete OAuth
For Shopify: generate a Private App API key in Shopify Admin and paste it here. For Amazon: use the Seller Central MWS authorisation flow. Each platform has on-screen instructions.
4
Test the sync
Click Sync Orders. New and pending orders will appear in Orders > Unprocessed within a few minutes. Confirm at least one test order has pulled through before going live.
Always test before go-live

Step 8 of 9

Configure Courier Settings

Control which couriers are active on your account and how WareIQ assigns them to orders. Auto-allocation works well for most brands — custom rules are for specific routing logic.

1
Go to Settings > Couriers
Click Settings in the left sidebar, then Couriers. Four tabs: Courier Allocation, All Couriers, Activated Couriers, Deactivated Couriers.
2
Leave Auto Shipping enabled (recommended)
The Auto Shipping toggle is on by default. WareIQ selects the best-cost serviceable courier per order automatically. Only disable if you want to select couriers manually for each order.
Recommended for new accounts
3
Activate or deactivate specific couriers
On the All Couriers tab, toggle off any courier you do not want WareIQ to use — for example, if you have a direct contract that bypasses this platform.
Optional
4
Create custom allocation rules (advanced)
Click Create Custom Rule to add routing logic by zone, weight, SKU, pincode, payment mode, order value, or channel. Rules are evaluated in priority order — place the most specific rules higher in the list.
Advanced — set up after first orders if needed
Example rule: "For all COD orders above Rs. 1,500 going to Zone A metro pincodes, use Delhivery." Set this under Courier Allocation > Custom Rules > + New Rule with Payment Mode = COD, Order Value > 1500, Zone = A, Courier = Delhivery.
Step 9 of 9

Complete Your Company Profile

Your company profile controls how your brand appears on customer tracking pages, shipping labels, and GST invoices. Complete this before generating your first label.

1
Go to Settings > Company Profile
Click Settings in the left sidebar and select Company Profile. Three tabs: Contact Details, Account Details, KYC Details.
2
Fill in Contact Details
Enter your Communication Name (the display name on tracking pages), Company Address, support email, and upload your Brand Logo. Recommended: 300×300px PNG with transparent background.
3
Verify Account Details and KYC tabs
Switch to Account Details to confirm your Client Code and Primary Email. Check KYC Details to confirm PAN, GST, and registered address are showing as approved.
4
Save your profile
Click Save at the bottom of the Contact Details tab. Changes apply to new orders — existing labels and invoices are not retroactively updated.
FieldWhere it appears
Communication Name RequiredBranded tracking page and customer-facing shipment emails.
Brand Logo OptionalTracking page header. PNG, max 2 MB, minimum 150px wide.
Support Email OptionalShown on tracking page as a customer contact option.
Client Code Read-onlyYour unique WareIQ account identifier. Needed when contacting support.
Branded tracking pages: Customers who click the tracking link in their shipping notification see your logo and Communication Name — not WareIQ branding. This reduces "where is my order" support tickets.

Your account is ready to ship

All 9 setup steps are complete. Create your first order, generate a label, and hand it to your courier partner.
